The Creation of Lua

In 1993, three members of a computer graphics group (Tecgraph) at the Pontifical Catholic University in Brazil created the Lua scripting language. These members were Roberto Ierusalimschy, Luiz Henrique de Figueiredo, and Waldemar Celes.

At this time, Brazil placed heavy restrictions on software imports to bolster it's computer science industry. This significantly impacted the workflow of Tecgraf, who required a scripting language that was practical given these restrictions. Additionally, the nature of these trade laws made the long-term accessibility of certain tools uncertain. Therefore, Lua was implemented as a highly-adaptable and light-weight language.

Core Issues Lua Solves

Lua is a scripting language. By definition, it integrates into another already-existent project to add automation, control behavior, and extend functionality. The specialty Lua has against other scripting langauges is its light-framework and accessibility.

Light-Framework:

  • simple language (21 keywords)
  • faster interpreter
  • compact size (fits small systems)

Accessibility:

  • flexible (custom metatables)
  • easy-to-link (function calls between C/C++)
  • adapts to team styles (multi-paradigm)

Lua In Today's Workspace

Lua is a very simple language to understand. It is frequently used by people that have no formal computer-science education. This is evident in the mods and add-ons made by certain gaming communities (Roblox, Garry's Mod, World of Warcraft). Additionally, it's frequently used in smart devices (Internet-of-Things) such as Raspberry Pi, fitness trackers, or smartwatches due to it's small size and fast runtime. Although not the main langauge in any project, Lua remains a very poewrful scripting tool that is desired for its ergonomics and accessibility to a broad audience.
